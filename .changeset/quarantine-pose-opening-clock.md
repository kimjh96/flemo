Glass-integrity fixes and an off-main image decode pipeline, all measured on a production consumer. The flight-time animation quarantine is now pose-preserving: consumer animations pin to their first-keyframe pose during the flight and rejoin their original clock at the landing, so delayed reveals never flash their base styles or restart their delay. An opening-clock guard covers the compiled CSS driver across the whole flight on non-Blink engines, rewinding spans a main-thread block advanced without presenting. Held arrivals keep their lazy images loading and the landing decodes them off-main before revealing. The diagnostic motion-driver force pin expires after 24 hours. And oversized images (sources carrying far more pixels than their layout box shows) are now automatically re-decoded to display size on a worker thread when CORS-readable and swapped in before their first paint (already-painted images are never touched) — WebKit's GPU-process path synchronously re-decodes full-resolution originals on the main thread on a recurring purge cycle (a single raw 37-megapixel avatar froze taps for ~380ms), and this gives it Blink's decode-to-scale pipeline at the page level: measured recurring stalls went from constant to zero.
